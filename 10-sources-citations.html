19  Sources & citations — the paper-writing reference

This chapter consolidates the citation chains for every paper-relevant column in analytic_wide.csv. The full column-by-column provenance lives in outputs/hansei_ichiran_reocr/data_provenance.md. This chapter is the narrative counterpart — what each source is, how it was acquired and verified, what variables it backs, and what to cite in the paper.

ImportantThe standing constraint

The paper cites Hansei Ichiran + published sources only. Two sources are routinely used inside the project as finders and sanity-checks but are never citable:

  • JP Wikipedia — used to locate primary-source candidates and to cross-check disputed cases, never as a citation. When a Wiki article cites Meiji Shiyō or 浅見 1994, the paper cites the underlying source.
  • bakumatsu.org — a Bakumatsu-era domain-listing website used for the initial domain_category and last_lord scrape, kept for internal cross-validation only.

When in doubt: if a value entered our dataset through one of those two channels and is not yet re-cited to a published source, it cannot be put into a paper table or regression sample.

19.1 The three source families

The dataset draws on three structurally different families of sources, each with its own role and citation pattern.

19.1.1 Family 1 — Hansei Ichiran 1870 (primary, project core)

The book itself: 藩制一覧 (Hansei Ichiran), 1870, two volumes, surveyed and compiled by the early Meiji government. Every hi_* column in analytic_wide.csv derives from this source, traced through the per-domain extracted chunks (extracted_chunk_vol1_t1.csv through vol2_t9.csv, plus four_domains_hansei_ichiran.csv for the hand-verified focal cases).

Citation form for the paper: Hansei Ichiran (藩制一覧), 1870, vol. N, table N, page N. Compiled by the Meiji-government survey of daimyo administrative returns.

The exact page reference is in the project’s MASTER record at MASTER_hansei_ichiran.csv (one row per per-domain per-field extraction, with the source crop / page noted in the source_crop column). When a paper table cites a specific HI number, the row-level source-crop reference establishes precisely where in the original document the value lives.

Volume / table mapping (per memory reference-hansei-ichiran-kanji):

  • Vol. 1 (T1–T5): identifier + revenue + population tables for most domains
  • Vol. 2 (T6–T9): military + fiscal detail. Saga (佐嘉藩) appears only in T6; no T7/T8/T9 entry (the reference-saga-HI-gap memory documents this)

19.1.2 Family 2 — Government primary documents (paper-citable)

Three contemporaneous government compilations that overlap with HI on population and revenue, plus the official ennoblement gazettes:

19.1.2.1 明治史要 (Meiji Shiyō)

Compiled by the 修史局 (Bureau of Historiography), this is the standard early-Meiji compendium of administrative data: domain populations, household counts, kokudaka, basic fiscal indicators. Our dataset uses Meiji Shiyō for the columns population_meiji_shiyo and households_meiji_shiyo.

Citation form: 明治史要 (Meiji Shiyō), Bureau of Historiography (修史局), early Meiji compendium.

19.1.2.2 大隈重信関係資料「府藩県石高人員表」

Held in the Ōkuma Shigenobu papers (大隈重信関係資料) at the Waseda University Library. The “府藩県石高人員表” is a contemporary tabulation of administrative-unit kokudaka and population. Backs the population_okuma and uchidaka_okuma_koku columns.

Citation form: 「府藩県石高人員表」, 大隈重信関係資料, Ōkuma Shigenobu papers, Waseda University Library archival collection.

19.1.2.3 統計集誌 (Tōkei Shūshi) “人員表”

Compiled by 呉文聡 from 野村文夫’s materials, published in Tōkei Shūshi (the early-Meiji statistical journal). Backs the population_tokei_shushi column.

Citation form: 統計集誌 (Tōkei Shūshi), 人員表, compiled by 呉文聡 from 野村文夫 materials.

19.1.2.4 官報 第307号 (1884-07-08) — primary ennoblement gazette (daimyo / sekke cohort)

The government gazette that recorded the 1884-07-07 ennoblement decree — the original 公侯伯子男 (prince/marquess/count/viscount/baron) rank assignments. Available via NDL Digital Collections (pid 2943511); the project downloaded the full PDF to outputs/ndl_kanpo_307/kanpo_307_1884-07-08.pdf (23 MB, 9 pages). This is the primary record underlying every kazoku_rank_actual value (with very few exceptions handled by Kanpō #308).

Citation form: 官報 第307号 (1884-07-08), NDL pid 2943511.

19.1.2.5 官報 第308号 (1884-07-09) — primary ennoblement gazette (late cohort)

The follow-up gazette for daimyo whose rank assignments lagged the main decree by one day. Backs a small number of kazoku_rank_actual rows including 牟原藩 (= 半原藩) and several other edge cases.

Citation form: 官報 第308号 (1884-07-09), NDL Digital Collections.

19.1.3 Family 3 — Scholarly synthesis (paper-citable)

Secondary scholarly works that the project cites not as the original record but as the synthesis-and-interpretation layer between the primary documents and our values.

19.1.3.1 浅見雅男『華族誕生 名誉と体面の明治』(リブロポート 1994)

The single most-important scholarly source for the paper’s central variable. Asami’s work:

  1. Reconstructs the 1884 Kazoku-rei threshold rule with sufficient detail that the project can compute the genmai-based mechanical-expected rank (kazoku_rank_expected_genmai) for every domain.
  2. Establishes 現米 (genmai) — not 草高 — as the threshold numerator. Pre-Asami sources (including Lebra 1993) implicitly used kokudaka as the threshold; Asami shows the rule explicitly operated on post-1869 genmai. This single point reduces the paper’s apparent “Boshin penalty” sample from 93 cases (Lebra framing) to 19 (Asami framing) by mechanically explaining 76 outcomes. The Sendai and Aizu cases that look like Restoration punishments turn out to be genmai-mechanical (see chapter 07).
  3. Documents the explicit exception clauses (sanke clause, Tokugawa-main-line clause, Restoration-leader clause, sankyō clause, post-1868-elevation clause) — each of which explains a specific subset of deviations from the standard rule.

The book is on Jed’s shelf. Pages 87–88 articulate the genmai rule; pp. 109–110 give the sanke and sankyō clauses; pp. 105–115 the Restoration-leader exception. Asami is the citation in 94% of kazoku_rank_source rows.

Citation form: 浅見雅男『華族誕生 名誉と体面の明治』リブロポート (1994).

19.1.3.2 Beasley, W.G. The Meiji Restoration. Stanford University Press (1972)

The standard English-language synthesis of the Restoration period. Used for:

  • Domain classifications — Beasley p. xii lists “Principal Domains in 1860” with explicit 親藩 / 譜代 / 外様 designations; this is the cross-check that confirmed 仙台 (Date) and 対馬府中 (Sō) as tozama against the bakumatsu.org mis-coding.
  • Boshin alignment — Beasley ch. XI pp. 295–299 provides the standard chronology of Toba-Fushimi and post-Toba-Fushimi imperial consolidation. The bulk boshin_alignment_source for ~213 small viscount-tier domains cites Beasley.
  • Hikone defection — Beasley p. 297 is the only English-language source that explicitly names Hikone as the pre-Toba-Fushimi defector (the Ii family broke with the bakufu after Ii Naosuke’s assassination in 1860 and the subsequent Sakuradamon Incident).

Citation form: Beasley, W.G. The Meiji Restoration. Stanford University Press (1972).

19.1.3.3 Esposito, Gabriele. The Boshin War 1868–69. Osprey Men-at-Arms 530 (2020)

A military-history reference with detailed coverage of the Boshin War combatants. Used for Boshin alignment citations on the major-domain combatants (the focal-4 plus the Northern Alliance core: Aizu/斗南, Sendai, Yonezawa, Shōnai, Nagaoka, Nihonmatsu, Morioka, Kuwana). Backs 10 boshin_alignment_source rows.

Citation form: Esposito, Gabriele. The Boshin War 1868-69. Osprey Men-at-Arms 530, Osprey Publishing (2020).

19.1.3.4 工藤威『奥羽列藩同盟の基礎的研究』(岩田書院 2002)

The standard Japanese-language academic monograph on the 奥羽越列藩同盟 (Ōuetsu Reppan Dōmei) — the Northern Alliance of bakumatsu domains that resisted the imperial advance after Toba-Fushimi. Backs the Boshin alignment citations for the 12 OREP combatants plus the 9 OREP defectors who switched sides mid-war. Surfaced 4 mis-classifications fixed in 2026-05-20 (守山, 館, 村上, 村松, 矢島 see chapter 02g).

Citation form: 工藤威『奥羽列藩同盟の基礎的研究』岩田書院 (2002).

19.1.3.5 児玉幸多 ed. 『藩史総覧』新人物往来社 (1977)

The canonical Japanese-language han-classification compendium. The principal source for domain_category (親藩 / 譜代 / 外様 / 御三卿 / 天領). Establishes formal category designations for all ~300 domains.

Citation form: 児玉幸多 編『藩史総覧』新人物往来社 (1977).

19.1.3.6 国史大辞典 (Yoshikawa Kōbunkan, 1979–1997)

The standard Japanese-language reference dictionary. Used as the secondary verification layer for individual house histories, daimyo biographies, and domain administrative changes. Not typically cited per row (since other sources back the specific values), but listed in the paper bibliography as the reference framework.

Citation form: 国史大辞典 (Yoshikawa Kōbunkan, 1979–1997 edition).

19.1.3.7 華族大鑑刊行会 編『華族大鑑』(1990)

The dedicated reference for the 1884 prince-rank (公爵) ennoblements specifically — used for the 3 prince cases in our dataset (Shimazu/Satsuma, Mōri/Chōshū, Tokugawa/Shizuoka) and as a supplementary citation alongside Asami for several marquess cases.

Citation form: 華族大鑑刊行会 編『華族大鑑』(1990).

19.1.3.8 小田部雄次『華族 近代日本貴族の虚像と実像』中公新書 (2006)

A modern general-reader-facing book on the kazoku system as a whole. Used in 12 small viscount-tier kazoku_rank_source rows from kazoku_rank_unmatched_16.csv.

Citation form: 小田部雄次『華族 近代日本貴族の虚像と実像』中公新書 (2006).

19.1.3.9 松田敬之『〈華族爵位〉請願人名辞典』吉川弘文館 (2015)

Definitive on the 1884 exclusions — daimyo families that were not ennobled because of the 改易 (kaieki, confiscation) or similar disqualifying events. Backs the documented exclusion of 堀江藩 (大沢家, 万石事件 1871) and the verification of 牟原藩 (= 半原藩, OCR-corrected). Three of the four NaN kazoku_rank_actual rows are exclusion cases documented per Matsuda 2015.

Citation form: 松田敬之『〈華族爵位〉請願人名辞典』吉川弘文館 (2015).

19.1.3.10 Mark Ravina. Land and Lordship in Early Modern Japan. Stanford University Press (1999)

Provides the dataset’s ratio variables (kusadaka_per_commoner, retainers_per_100_commoners, gendaka_per_commoner, retainers_per_1000_koku, gendaka_to_kusadaka_ratio) from Appendix Table A1 of the book. 227 rows in our dataset have Ravina coverage. One confirmed typo: Ravina A1’s value for Akita 久保田藩 gendaka_per_commoner is 0.049 but the primary-derived value is 0.49 (decimal-place error). See memory reference-ravina-akita-typo.

Citation form: Mark Ravina. Land and Lordship in Early Modern Japan. Stanford University Press (1999), Appendix Table A1.

19.1.3.11 Focal-4 published-source supplements

For each of the focal-4 domains (Saga, Satsuma, Chōshū, Tosa) the project compiled a published-source supplement listing citable references for military / political / institutional details that go beyond what Hansei Ichiran provides:

  • outputs/hansei_ichiran_reocr/saga_published_sources.md — cites 藤野保『佐賀藩』(2010), Jentschura 1977 (warships), Beasley 1972
  • outputs/hansei_ichiran_reocr/satsuma_published_sources.md — Esposito 2020, Jentschura 1977, Beasley 1972, Craig 1961/2000
  • outputs/hansei_ichiran_reocr/choshu_published_sources.md — Craig 1961/2000, Jansen 1961/1994
  • outputs/hansei_ichiran_reocr/tosa_published_sources.md — Roberts 1998, Jansen 1961/1994

Each supplement is a paper-writing-ready citation block for that focal domain’s narrative section.

19.3 The Boshin alignment citation chain

The boshin_alignment_source column has a hierarchical citation pattern reflecting the difficulty of citing a single source for all 287 domains:

Category Citation pattern Count
Major combatants (10 domains) Esposito 2020 + Beasley 1972 ch. XI + Boshin War Wikipedia (as finder) 10
OREP combatants (12 domains) 工藤威 2002 + JP Wiki 奥羽越列藩同盟 + Beasley 1972 p. 298 12
OREP defectors (9 domains) 工藤威 2002 + JP Wiki 9
Toba-Fushimi-era defectors (4 domains) Various — Beasley p. 297 (Hikone), JP Wiki 淀藩 (Yodo), Esposito (Tsu), Beasley silence + JP Wiki (Kaga) 4
Tokugawa-affiliate non-OREP (~20 domains) “Standard Boshin chronology per Beasley 1972 ch. XI; family-affiliation Tokugawa-loyalist” ~20
Anti-bakufu bulk (~213 small viscount-tier) “Post-Toba-Fushimi imperial consolidation per Beasley 1972 ch. XI pp. 295–299” ~213
Neutral bulk (~10 domains) “Standard Boshin chronology per Beasley 1972 ch. XI” ~10
Total 287 ✅

The bulk-citation pattern (the 213 anti-bakufu + 20 Tokugawa-affiliate + 10 neutral) is a single Beasley-1972-chapter-XI reference that frames the standard chronology rather than enumerating each domain individually. Beasley’s analysis is that after Toba-Fushimi the imperial side issued a general directive and most small domains complied without active combatant role. Our coding follows this — listing them anti-bakufu by end-state rather than by Boshin participation.

19.5 NOT citable — internal sanity-check sources

The dataset retains two non-citable sources for internal validation only. Neither appears in any paper-citable column; both are used purely to cross-check values and to seed lookups against citable primary sources.

19.5.1 JP Wikipedia (and English Wikipedia)

Used as a finder to: - Locate primary-source candidates (e.g., a Wikipedia article cites Meiji Shiyō → check the underlying Meiji Shiyō value) - Cross-validate values that already have a citable source (e.g., Asami 1994 says marquess; JP Wiki article on the family also says marquess — confirms transcription) - Initial seed for last_lord and provincial location fields (paper-relevant) — but these need a primary citation chain to be paper-citable

Wikipedia is never citable in the paper. Per memory feedback-sourcing-constraint, the paper cites the underlying primary sources that Wikipedia references, not Wikipedia itself.

19.5.2 bakumatsu.org (内部 web scrape)

Used as an internal cross-validation layer for: - domain_category initial coding (now superseded by 児玉 1977 + Beasley 1972 re-cite as of session 2 of this project) - boshin_alignment_group initial coding (now superseded by per-row citation chain as of session 2) - last_lord and provincial fields (still partially internal-only; would need re-cite for paper inclusion)

bakumatsu.org is never citable. It is preserved in the project only because the initial scrape was the spine that allowed the bulk-data ingestion to bootstrap. All paper-relevant fields have since been re-cited.
