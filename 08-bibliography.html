Bibliography

This page aggregates every published source the dataset draws on. For per-row citations, see the matching source columns in the analytic table.

Primary sources

藩制一覧 (Hansei Ichiran, “Overview of Han Institutions”). 1870. Two-volume government survey of all daimyō (大名) domains, compiled in the immediate pre-haihan chiken (廃藩置県) period. National Diet Library Digital Collections. — The primary han-level source underlying every Hansei Ichiran-derived field.

官報 (Kanpō, “Official Gazette”) 第307号 (Kanpō #307). 1884-07-08. The Imperial government gazette publishing the 1884-07-07 kazoku (華族) ennoblement decree and initial 509-house roster. NDL Digital Collections, pid 2943511. — Primary citation for the 1884 kazoku* rank.*

官報 第308号 (Kanpō #308). 1884-07-09. Continuation of the same gazette with the late-cohort ennoblements (kuge (公家), etc.). NDL pid 2943512.

明治史要 (Meiji Shiyō, “Outline of Meiji History”). 1870s. Compendium compiled by the Bureau of Historiography (修史局, Shūshikyoku). — Primary source for the Meiji Shiyō population, household-count, and status-class breakdown fields, and for the verbatim 1871 haihan-chiken imperial decree (vol 1 pp. 27–28) used by the focal-han tier columns.

大隈重信関係資料「府藩県石高人口表」 (Ōkuma Shigenobu kankei shiryō: Fu-han-ken kokudaka jinkō-hyō, “Ōkuma Shigenobu Papers: Prefecture-Domain-County Kokudaka and Population Table”). Waseda University Library archival, call number イ14-A4361, Meiji 2 (1869). — Primary source for the Ōkuma uchidaka and population fields.

統計集誌 (Tōkei Shūshi, “Statistical Journal”). Compiled by 呉文聡 from 野村文夫 materials. — Primary source for the Tōkei Shūshi population field.

世外井上公伝編纂会編『世外井上公伝』全5巻 (Sekkai Inoue-kō Den Compilation Committee (ed.), Sekkai Inoue-kō Den, “Authorized Biography of Inoue Kaoru”). 1933–34. — Vol 1 pp. 449–453 (Ch. 3 §1 廃藩置縣) gives Inoue Kaoru’s actor-perspective on haihan-chiken (1871) including the pre-decree negotiations, the July 9 Kudan-zaka implementation council, Inoue’s 1909 Kyō-Meikai retrospective, and the verbatim two-tier focal-domain commendation (Sat-Chō-Saga-Tosa + Kumamoto/Nagoya/Tokushima/Tottori). Used by the focal-han tier columns.

木戸孝允『木戸孝允日記』(English: The Diary of Kido Takayoshi, trans. Sidney Devere Brown & Akiko Hirota, University of Tokyo Press, 1983). — Vol II pp. 65–67 contains Kido’s diary entries for 28–31 August 1871 (Meiji 4/7/13–16) on haihan-chiken, including his retrospective claim to having originated the 郡県制 reform, the “fiefs from Tokugawa-enemy” stratagem, the focal-four sequential persuasion narrative, and the rejected “hereditary governor” compromise. Vol III pp. 344–347 contains the 3–7 August 1876 entries on the chitsuroku-shobun debate (Kido vs Itō). Used by the focal-han tier columns and the chitsuroku-shobun chapter.

大久保利通家編『大久保利通文書』全10巻 (Ōkubo Toshimichi Family (ed.), Ōkubo Toshimichi Monjo, “Ōkubo Toshimichi Documents”). 日本史籍協會, 1927–29. — Authoritative chronological compilation of Ōkubo’s correspondence, 1851–1878. Vol 1 covers the pre-Restoration period (Kaei 4 / 1851 → Keiō 3 / Oct 1867); entry 124’s reference document is Ōkubo’s hand-copy of the Satsuma-Tosa Treaty (薩土盟約, Keiō 3/6/14 = July 1867), the founding political compact of the Sat-Chō-Tosa coalition that became the focal four (with Hizen joining) in 1871. Vol 4 (1928, NDL pid 1075767) covers Meiji 3/9 → Meiji 5; 卷二十一 (Internal Vol 21) contains entries 623–630 + the 利通日記抄 diary excerpts for Meiji 4/7 — Ōkubo’s contemporaneous record of the 7/9 Kudan-zaka implementation council and the 7/14 day-of-decree entry. The 1928 editorial 【辨説】 commentary documents the Kido vs Ōkubo intra-oligarchy disagreement on the 4-han cabinet balance. Vols 2, 3, 5 also acquired via NDL Digital Collections.

太政官布告 第108号 + 第109号 (Daijōkan-fukoku Nos. 108 & 109). 1876-08-05. The legal-government primary for the chitsuroku-shobun (秩禄処分). No. 108 = 金禄公債證書発行条例 (Hereditary-Stipend Public-Bond Certificate Issuance Ordinance) — the 7-article ordinance establishing the bond conversion schedule (regressive multipliers × progressive interest), 5-year grace + 30-year lottery amortization. No. 109 (same day) authorizes secondary-market trading. Located in 法令全書 明治9年 (NDL pid 787956, PDM, internet-public), book pp. 147–151. — The chitsuroku-shobun legal primary, parallels Kanpō #307’s role for the kazoku-rei. Sat-Chō-Saga-Tosa former daimyō all sit in the least-generous conversion tier (≥30k yen → 5–6 years’ worth × 5% interest).

法令全書 (Hōrei Zensho, “Compendium of Laws and Ordinances”), 内閣官報局 (Cabinet Printing Bureau), Meiji 9 vol. = 1876 ed. NDL pid 787956. — Canonical compiled-laws series; contains all 太政官布告 + 太政官達 of Meiji 9. Source for the chitsuroku-shobun decrees above.

Secondary scholarly sources — Japanese

浅見雅男『華族誕生: 名誉と体面の明治』 (Asami Masao, Kazoku tanjō: meiyo to taimen no Meiji, “Birth of the Peerage: Honor and Reputation in Meiji”). リブロポート, 1994. — Backs 94% of the 1884 kazoku-rank citations. Articulates the genmai* (現米) threshold rule that reduces apparent rank-deviations from 81 (kokudaka-based) to 19 (genmai-based).*

華族大鑑刊行会編『華族大鑑』 (Kazoku Taikan Kankōkai (ed.), Kazoku taikan, “Compendium of the Peerage”). 1990. — Primary scholarly source for the dataset’s prince-rank ennoblements (3 rows).

工藤威『奥羽列藩同盟の基礎的研究 (Ōu reppan dōmei no kisoteki kenkyū, “Basic Research on the Northern Alliance”)』. 岩田書院, 2002. — Authoritative scholarly roster of the Northern Alliance (Ōuetsu Reppan Dōmei), 31 members. Citable via the JP Wikipedia 奥羽越列藩同盟 article which transcribes the Kudō roster.

児玉幸多 ed.『藩史総覧 (Hanshi sōran, “Comprehensive Survey of Han History”)』. 新人物往来社, 1977. — Canonical han-classification reference for the Tokugawa-era category field (shinpan* (親藩) / fudai (譜代) / tozama (外様) / sankyō (御三卿) / tenryō (天領)).*

国史大辞典 (Kokushi daijiten, “Great Dictionary of National History”). Yoshikawa Kōbunkan, 1979–1997. — Definitive secondary on Japanese historical terminology + domain classifications.

小田部雄次『華族 近代日本貴族の虚像と実像 (Kazoku: kindai Nihon kizoku no kyozō to jitsuzō, “The Peerage: Image and Reality of the Modern Japanese Aristocracy”)』. 中公新書, 2006. — Secondary kazoku* reference, especially for small-domain edge cases.*

森岡浩『日本名門・名家大辞典 (Nihon meimon meika daijiten, “Great Dictionary of Famous Japanese Houses”)』. 東京堂出版, 2012. — Small-domain rank references.

松田敬之『〈華族爵位〉請願人名辞典 (Kazoku shakui seigan jinmei jiten, “Dictionary of Peerage-Title Petitioners”)』. 吉川弘文館, 2015. — Definitive 1884 exclusions catalog (kaieki* (改易) cases like 大沢家/堀江藩, 林家/請西藩).*

藤野保『佐賀藩』. 2010. — Saga-specific reference; supplies Saga’s navy, which Hansei Ichiran’s Volume 2 does not record (Saga’s army is in Table 9).

Secondary scholarly sources — English

Beasley, W. G. The Meiji Restoration. Stanford University Press, 1972. — Standard scholarly history. Ch. XI for Boshin alignment + Principal Domains in 1860 (p. xii) for Tokugawa-era category assignments.

Esposito, Gabriele & Rava, Giuseppe. Japanese Armies 1868–1877: The Boshin War and Satsuma Rebellion. Osprey Men-at-Arms 530. Bloomsbury, 2020. — Military narrative + key Boshin combatants. Citable for the major focal-4 + Northern Alliance core.

Lebra, Takie Sugiyama. Above the Clouds: Status Culture of the Modern Japanese Nobility. University of Hawai’i Press, 1993. — Threshold-rule framing (kokudaka-based, which Asami 1994 corrects to genmai-based).

Ravina, Mark. Land and Lordship in Early Modern Japan. Stanford University Press, 1999. — Appendix Table A1 ratios. Cited for the per-capita ratio fields. Known typo: Akita 久保田藩 gendaka* (現高)-per-commoner 0.049 should be 0.49 (decimal-place error).*

Craig, A. M. Chōshū in the Meiji Restoration. Harvard University Press, 1961 / 2000. — Chōshū-specific military supplement.

Roberts, Luke S. Mercantilism in a Japanese Domain: The Merchant Origins of Economic Nationalism in 18th-Century Tosa. Cambridge University Press, 1998. — Tosa supplement.

Jansen, Marius B. The Making of Modern Japan. Harvard / Belknap, 2000. — General history; also Jansen (1961/1994) Sakamoto Ryōma and the Meiji Restoration.

Drea, Edward J. Japan’s Imperial Army: Its Rise and Fall, 1853–1945. University Press of Kansas, 2009.

Jentschura, H., Jung, D. & Mickel, P. Warships of the Imperial Japanese Navy, 1869–1945. Naval Institute Press, 1977. — For Hansei Ichiran warship data verification.

Data sources (not citable but documented)

Harvard CHGIS 1664 daimyō shapefile. — Used for the map polygons (approximation, since 1664 boundaries were generally stable through 1870).

bakumatsu.org web scrape. — Originally used as a finder for Tokugawa-era category, Boshin alignment, and last-lord identity. Not directly citable; values were cross-validated against Beasley 1972, 工藤 2002, 児玉 ed. 1977 and similar academic sources before being used in any analytic claim.

Wikipedia 府藩県三治制 scrape. — A web-scraped extract of the Japanese Wikipedia 府藩県三治制 article. Not directly citable; the article’s underlying primary sources (Meiji Shiyō, Ōkuma papers, Tōkei Shūshi) are the actual citations for each field.
