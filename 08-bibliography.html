Bibliography

This page aggregates every published source the dataset draws on. For per-row citations, see the matching source columns in the analytic table.

Primary sources

Hansei Ichiran (藩制一覧はんせいいちらん). 1870. Two-volume government survey of all daimyo domains, compiled in the immediate pre-haihan-chiken period. National Diet Library Digital Collections. — The primary han-level source underlying every HI-derived field.

官報かんぽう 第307号 (Kanpō #307). 1884-07-08. The Imperial government gazette publishing the 1884-07-07 kazoku ennoblement decree and initial 509-house roster. NDL Digital Collections, pid 2943511. — Primary citation for the 1884 kazoku rank.

官報かんぽう 第308号 (Kanpō #308). 1884-07-09. Continuation of the same gazette with the late-cohort ennoblements (kuge, etc.). NDL pid 2943512.

明治史要めいじしよう (Meiji Shiyō). 1870s. Compendium compiled by the Bureau of Historiography (修史局しゅうしきょく). — Primary source for the Meiji Shiyō population, household-count, and status-class breakdown fields.

大隈重信おおくましげのぶ関係資料かんけいしりょう「府藩県石高人員表ふはんけんこくだかじんいんひょう」 (Ōkuma Shigenobu papers — Fu-Han-Ken Kokudaka Jin’in-hyō). Waseda University Library archival. — Primary source for the Ōkuma uchidaka and population fields.

統計集誌とうけいしゅうし (Tōkei Shūshi). Compiled by 呉文聡くれぶんそう from 野村文夫のむらふみお materials. — Primary source for the Tōkei Shūshi population field.

Secondary scholarly sources — Japanese

浅見雅男あさみまさお『華族誕生かぞくたんじょう 名誉と体面の明治めいよとたいめんのめいじ』. リブロポートりぶろぽーと, 1994. — Backs 94% of the 1884 kazoku-rank citations. Articulates the 現米げんまい (genmai) threshold rule that reduces apparent rank-deviations from 93 (kokudaka-based) to 19 (genmai-based).

華族大鑑刊行会かぞくたいかんかんこうかい編『華族大鑑かぞくたいかん』. 1990. — Primary scholarly source for prince-rank ennoblements (20 rows).

工藤威くどうたけし『奥羽列藩同盟おううれっぱんどうめいの基礎的研究きそてきけんきゅう』. 岩田書院いわたしょいん, 2002. — Authoritative scholarly roster of the Northern Alliance (Ōuetsu Reppan Dōmei), 31 members. Citable via the JP Wikipedia 奥羽越列藩同盟おううえつれっぱんどうめい article which transcribes the Kudō roster.

児玉幸多こだまこうた ed.『藩史総覧はんしそうらん』. 新人物往来社しんじんぶつおうらいしゃ, 1977. — Canonical han-classification reference for the Tokugawa-era category field (親藩しんぱん / 譜代ふだい / 外様とざま / 御三卿ごさんきょう / 天領てんりょう).

国史大辞典こくしだいじてん. Yoshikawa Kōbunkan, 1979–1997. — Definitive secondary on Japanese historical terminology + domain classifications.

小田部雄次おたべゆうじ『華族かぞく 近代日本貴族の虚像と実像きんだいにほんきぞくのきょぞうとじつぞう』. 中公新書ちゅうこうしんしょ, 2006. — Secondary kazoku reference, especially for small-domain edge cases.

森岡浩もりおかひろし『日本名門にほんめいもん・名家大辞典めいかだいじてん』. 東京堂出版とうきょうどうしゅっぱん, 2012. — Small-domain rank references.

松田敬之まつだたかゆき『〈華族爵位かぞくしゃくい〉請願人名辞典せいがんじんめいじてん』. 吉川弘文館よしかわこうぶんかん, 2015. — Definitive 1884 exclusions catalog (改易かいえき cases like 大沢家おおさわけ/堀江藩ほりえはん, 林家はやしけ/請西じょうざい藩はん).

藤野保ふじのたもつ『佐賀藩さがはん』. 2010. — Saga-specific reference (used because 佐嘉藩さがはん is absent from HI vol. 2 T7–T9).

Secondary scholarly sources — English

Beasley, W. G. The Meiji Restoration. Stanford University Press, 1972. — Standard scholarly history. Ch. XI for Boshin alignment + Principal Domains in 1860 (p. xii) for Tokugawa-era category assignments.

Esposito, Gabriele & Rava, Giuseppe. Japanese Armies 1868–1877: The Boshin War and Satsuma Rebellion. Osprey Men-at-Arms 530. Bloomsbury, 2020. — Military narrative + key Boshin combatants. Citable for the major focal-4 + Northern Alliance core.

Lebra, Takie Sugiyama. Above the Clouds: Status Culture of the Modern Japanese Nobility. University of Hawai’i Press, 1993. — Threshold-rule framing (kokudaka-based, which Asami 1994 corrects to genmai-based).

Ravina, Mark. Land and Lordship in Early Modern Japan. Stanford University Press, 1999. — Appendix Table A1 ratios. Cited for the per-capita ratio fields. Known typo: Akita 久保田藩くぼたはん gendaka-per-commoner 0.049 should be 0.49 (decimal-place error).

Craig, A. M. Choshu in the Meiji Restoration. Harvard University Press, 1961 / 2000. — Choshu-specific military supplement.

Roberts, Luke S. Mercantilism in a Japanese Domain: The Merchant Origins of Economic Nationalism in 18th-Century Tosa. Cambridge University Press, 1998. — Tosa supplement.

Jansen, Marius B. The Making of Modern Japan. Harvard / Belknap, 2000. — General history; also Jansen (1961/1994) Sakamoto Ryōma and the Meiji Restoration.

Drea, Edward J. Japan’s Imperial Army: Its Rise and Fall, 1853–1945. University Press of Kansas, 2009.

Jentschura, H., Jung, D. & Mickel, P. Warships of the Imperial Japanese Navy, 1869–1945. Naval Institute Press, 1977. — For HI warship data verification.

Data sources (not citable but documented)

Harvard CHGIS 1664 daimyo shapefile. — Used for the map polygons (approximation, since 1664 boundaries were generally stable through 1870).

bakumatsu.org web scrape. — Originally used as a finder for Tokugawa-era category, Boshin alignment, and last-lord identity. Not directly citable; values were cross-validated against Beasley 1972, 工藤くどう 2002, 児玉こだま ed. 1977 and similar academic sources before being used in any analytic claim.

Wikipedia 府藩県三治制ふはんけんさんちせい scrape. — A web-scraped extract of the Japanese Wikipedia 府藩県三治制ふはんけんさんちせい article. Not directly citable; the article’s underlying primary sources (Meiji Shiyō, Ōkuma papers, Tōkei Shūshi) are the actual citations for each field.
