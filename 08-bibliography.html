Bibliography

This page aggregates every published source the dataset draws on. For per-row citations, see the matching source columns in the analytic table.

Primary sources

Hansei Ichiran (藩制一覧). 1870. Two-volume government survey of all daimyo domains, compiled in the immediate pre-haihan-chiken period. National Diet Library Digital Collections. — The primary han-level source underlying every HI-derived field.

官報 第307号 (Kanpō #307). 1884-07-08. The Imperial government gazette publishing the 1884-07-07 kazoku ennoblement decree and initial 509-house roster. NDL Digital Collections, pid 2943511. — Primary citation for the 1884 kazoku rank.

官報 第308号 (Kanpō #308). 1884-07-09. Continuation of the same gazette with the late-cohort ennoblements (kuge, etc.). NDL pid 2943512.

明治史要 (Meiji Shiyō). 1870s. Compendium compiled by the Bureau of Historiography (修史局). — Primary source for the Meiji Shiyō population, household-count, and status-class breakdown fields.

大隈重信関係資料「府藩県石高人員表」 (Ōkuma Shigenobu papers — Fu-Han-Ken Kokudaka Jin’in-hyō). Waseda University Library archival. — Primary source for the Ōkuma uchidaka and population fields.

統計集誌 (Tōkei Shūshi). Compiled by 呉文聡 from 野村文夫 materials. — Primary source for the Tōkei Shūshi population field.

Secondary scholarly sources — Japanese

浅見雅男『華族誕生 名誉と体面の明治』. リブロポート, 1994. — Backs 94% of the 1884 kazoku-rank citations. Articulates the 現米 (genmai) threshold rule that reduces apparent rank-deviations from 93 (kokudaka-based) to 19 (genmai-based).

華族大鑑刊行会編『華族大鑑』. 1990. — Primary scholarly source for prince-rank ennoblements (20 rows).

工藤威『奥羽列藩同盟の基礎的研究』. 岩田書院, 2002. — Authoritative scholarly roster of the Northern Alliance (Ōuetsu Reppan Dōmei), 31 members. Citable via the JP Wikipedia 奥羽越列藩同盟 article which transcribes the Kudō roster.

児玉幸多 ed.『藩史総覧』. 新人物往来社, 1977. — Canonical han-classification reference for the Tokugawa-era category field (親藩 / 譜代 / 外様 / 御三卿 / 天領).

国史大辞典. Yoshikawa Kōbunkan, 1979–1997. — Definitive secondary on Japanese historical terminology + domain classifications.

小田部雄次『華族 近代日本貴族の虚像と実像』. 中公新書, 2006. — Secondary kazoku reference, especially for small-domain edge cases.

森岡浩『日本名門・名家大辞典』. 東京堂出版, 2012. — Small-domain rank references.

松田敬之『〈華族爵位〉請願人名辞典』. 吉川弘文館, 2015. — Definitive 1884 exclusions catalog (改易 cases like 大沢家/堀江藩, 林家/請西藩).

藤野保『佐賀藩』. 2010. — Saga-specific reference (used because 佐嘉藩 is absent from HI vol. 2 T7–T9).

Secondary scholarly sources — English

Beasley, W. G. The Meiji Restoration. Stanford University Press, 1972. — Standard scholarly history. Ch. XI for Boshin alignment + Principal Domains in 1860 (p. xii) for Tokugawa-era category assignments.

Esposito, Gabriele & Rava, Giuseppe. Japanese Armies 1868–1877: The Boshin War and Satsuma Rebellion. Osprey Men-at-Arms 530. Bloomsbury, 2020. — Military narrative + key Boshin combatants. Citable for the major focal-4 + Northern Alliance core.

Lebra, Takie Sugiyama. Above the Clouds: Status Culture of the Modern Japanese Nobility. University of Hawai’i Press, 1993. — Threshold-rule framing (kokudaka-based, which Asami 1994 corrects to genmai-based).

Ravina, Mark. Land and Lordship in Early Modern Japan. Stanford University Press, 1999. — Appendix Table A1 ratios. Cited for the per-capita ratio fields. Known typo: Akita 久保田藩 gendaka-per-commoner 0.049 should be 0.49 (decimal-place error).

Craig, A. M. Choshu in the Meiji Restoration. Harvard University Press, 1961 / 2000. — Choshu-specific military supplement.

Roberts, Luke S. Mercantilism in a Japanese Domain: The Merchant Origins of Economic Nationalism in 18th-Century Tosa. Cambridge University Press, 1998. — Tosa supplement.

Jansen, Marius B. The Making of Modern Japan. Harvard / Belknap, 2000. — General history; also Jansen (1961/1994) Sakamoto Ryōma and the Meiji Restoration.

Drea, Edward J. Japan’s Imperial Army: Its Rise and Fall, 1853–1945. University Press of Kansas, 2009.

Jentschura, H., Jung, D. & Mickel, P. Warships of the Imperial Japanese Navy, 1869–1945. Naval Institute Press, 1977. — For HI warship data verification.

Data sources (not citable but documented)

Harvard CHGIS 1664 daimyo shapefile. — Used for the map polygons (approximation, since 1664 boundaries were generally stable through 1870).

bakumatsu.org web scrape. — Originally used as a finder for Tokugawa-era category, Boshin alignment, and last-lord identity. Not directly citable; values were cross-validated against Beasley 1972, 工藤 2002, 児玉 ed. 1977 and similar academic sources before being used in any analytic claim.

Wikipedia 府藩県三治制 scrape. — A web-scraped extract of the Japanese Wikipedia 府藩県三治制 article. Not directly citable; the article’s underlying primary sources (Meiji Shiyō, Ōkuma papers, Tōkei Shūshi) are the actual citations for each field.
