22  Data quality and the audit framework

Any analytic result built on analytic_wide.csv is only as defensible as the values it ingests. This chapter walks the four-layer audit framework used to build confidence in the dataset, the cumulative reduction in known issues, the high-impact fixes that landed, and what remains uncertain — documented openly rather than hidden.

ImportantThe headline numbers
  • Master extraction record: 7,161 rows; 96.5 percent high-confidence (6,913 rows); 63 medium-confidence; 42 low; 143 not applicable.
  • Honest-medium tracker: started at 141 flagged rows; reduced through successive cleanup passes to 22 rows, all of which are structural source-level limits that no further OCR work can resolve.
  • Within-domain sanity-check flags: started at 37; reduced to 5 after the 2026-05-27 audit corrected 森藩’s four 戸數/大豆 leading-digit errors (which had produced an impossible 0.99 persons/household ratio) and resolved the 庄内/湯長谷/新庄 人員 (status_class) prefix-match bug. The remaining 5 flags are documented as definitional (人吉藩’s high sotsu ratio is real; 仙台’s 1.72 ratio reflects post-Boshin household-stratification quirks; etc.).
  • Cross-page contamination audits: 4 cases confirmed and fixed in the May 2026 cleanup passes; an additional 3 open cases (upper-p151, lower-p60, upper-p81) re-verified clean in the 2026-05-27 audit. A fuzzy near-match audit found 8 candidates, all benign; a complete cross-domain value-collision check of 1,250 candidates surfaced 1 real error (姫路’s sōdaka) plus 25 confirmed coincidences. See section “2d — The 2026-05-27 systematic audit” below.
  • Citation coverage on paper-critical variables: 100 percent on the three paper controls (kazoku (華族) rank, Boshin alignment, domain category); 99 percent on kazoku_rank_actual (the 3 missing values are documented exclusions, not data gaps).

The paper-critical variables are all paper-ready. The remaining medium-confidence rows affect tail-end fiscal precision and hyper-specific demographic sub-counts that aren’t used in any paper table or regression.

22.1 The four audit layers

22.1.1 Layer 1 — Within-domain ratio sanity

For each domain the audit computes ratios that ought to make demographic sense and flags any that don’t:

Ratio Plausible range Flag trigger
Average household size (人口 / 戸數) 3–7 persons Outside 3–7
shizoku (士族) persons per household 3–8 persons Outside 3–8
sotsu (卒族) persons per household 3–8 persons Outside 3–8
Population per koku 0.5–5 persons / koku Outside 0.5–5
Status-class share of total population Each less than 50% Implausible class shares

When a flag fires, the audit identifies a specific root cause: OCR misread, mislabelling (sub-total mistaken for total), cross-page contamination, or genuine demographic anomaly.

Notable resolutions from this audit:

Domain Issue Resolution
一宮藩 人口 = 3,201 (impossibly far below the population-per-koku floor) OCR misread of 14,240; corrected via crop re-read
加納藩 戸數 = 317 reported as the domain total Was actually the 城下町 (castle-town) subset; relabelled
亀田藩 shizoku population = 23,258 Subtotal mislabelled; correct domain-wide total of 23,894 added
成羽藩 人口 = 494 Was 人員(寺領分) — temple-land subset; relabelled
吉見藩 人口 = 372 Was 人員 士卒族 — combined sub-class subset; relabelled
仙台藩 A “兵卒 18,913 persons” row that contained 5,171 women Was the sotsu (sotsu-class) household population, not active troops; relabelled. Active troops corrected to 14,262.
前橋藩 shizoku household count equal to shizoku person count (4,656) Alias-rule regression — the bare “shizoku” prefix matched “shizoku (人口)”; alias rule corrected

The audit started at 37 flags and dropped to 26 after these fixes plus the contamination resolutions below. The remaining 26 flags are documented as definitional (for example 人吉藩’s high sotsu ratio is real: Hitoyoshi-Sagara had a vast sotsu-class peasant-soldier population, confirmed against Meiji Shiyō).

22.1.2 Layer 2 — Cross-page contamination audits

Hansei Ichiran is a printed book; many of its pages contain two domains stacked vertically (upper and lower halves of a page). The extraction pipeline assigns rows to one canonical domain per page based on the visible 藩 header. When a page has two domains, the assignment can drift — values from the lower domain accidentally tagged as the upper, or vice versa.

22.1.2.1 2a — Exact-match audit

The exact-match audit identifies pages containing two or more domains, then checks whether numeric values match exactly across the suspect pairs. 261 PDF pages have two or more domains. The audit found 22 value-collisions; 4 were confirmed contamination and fixed:

Page Pre-fix issue Resolution
upper-p87 和歌山 / 金澤 11 Wakayama rows duplicated Kanazawa data (Wakayama’s sōdaka (草高) wrongly showed 1,353,352 — Kanazawa’s value) Removed; Wakayama now correctly shows sōdaka = 539,469 (true Kii kokudaka (石高))
upper-p151 津山 / 津和野 6 Tsuyama rows were a mix of Tsu (人口 247k) and Tsuwano (sōdaka 75k) All 6 removed; Tsuyama’s true sōdaka = 104,576 was already on p150 (unaffected)
lower-p60 岸和田 / 菊間 7 Kishiwada rows duplicated Kikuma; Kishiwada’s actual data on p58 had never been extracted 7 wrong rows removed; 12 verified Kishiwada rows added from the correct crop
upper-p81 大溝 / 岡田 岡田藩’s 人口 = 11,433 was actually Ōmizo’s value; Okada’s real population (16,231) had never been extracted 1 wrong row removed; 6 verified Okada rows added from the correct crop

22.1.2.2 2b — Fuzzy near-match audit

A complementary methodology with 0.5 percent relative tolerance — catches contamination where the values aren’t exactly equal but are very close (for example two adjacent 10,000-koku-tier domains reporting similar values). Found 8 candidates; all 8 verified as benign:

  • 5 cases: sequential book page numbers showing up as similar values across nearby pages — incidental, not data contamination.
  • 1 case: small-number coincidence (both values in the 100–200 range).
  • 1 case: Tateyama vs Tawaramoto (both 10,000-koku domains) — confirmed as legitimate independent values that happen to be coincidentally close.
  • 1 case: Himeji vs Hikone sōdaka (草高) tally of 250,000 was initially classified as a benign coincidence (both very large fudai sub-territory tallies) but the 2026-05-27 audit re-investigated this finding and confirmed it as a real transcription error: Himeji’s MASTER raw read 二十五萬石 元高 (= 250,000), but the actual page printed 拾五萬石 (= 150,000). The internal-arithmetic consistency check is dispositive — Himeji’s same-block 合 高 of 212,124 cannot be produced by 250,000 + any positive 外 高. Corrected 2026-05-27.

22.1.2.3 2c — The Tosa–Satsuma page-spill (right-of-marker)

The most historically instructive contamination. Tosa’s hi_jinkou had carried 896,808, essentially identical to Satsuma’s population (Tōkei Shūshi 896,817). The 2026-05 right-of-marker sweep showed this was not an OCR/tabulation column-stack but a physical page-spill: Satsuma’s entire demographic block — 174,528 households / 896,808 persons, 士族 43,119 / 102,949, plus the 琉球 (Ryūkyū) tribute — is printed to the right of the ○高知藩 marker on book p152 (upper_p089_R), i.e. as the tail of the preceding domain (Satsuma), whose own page records no demographics. Re-attributed to Satsuma; Tosa’s real Hansei Ichiran population — 495,936, on the facing page upper_p090_R — was recovered and now populates hi_jinkou, and Satsuma’s demographics were populated for the first time. Twin of the 山口/黒川 case; Meiji Shiyō’s 516,867 remains a close cross-check for Tosa.

22.1.2.4 2d — The 2026-05-27 systematic audit

A complete cross-domain value-collision audit was run in May 2026 across all 286 domains × all numeric Hansei Ichiran columns. The audit started with 1,250 raw cross-domain matches; after filtering out coincidences shared by more than two domains (the small-bakumatsu kokudaka coincidences at 10,000 / 12,000 / 27,000 / etc.) the candidate set was 57; after individual crop verification of all 26 unique cells the result was:

  • 1 real transcription error: 姫路藩 sōdaka — see section 2b above for detail. Fixed.
  • 25 confirmed coincidences: small-domain populations or household counts that numerically coincide with another domain’s kokudaka — cross-metric matches that are statistically expected at the magnitudes involved.

The same audit run also resolved the three open cross-page contamination cases (upper-p151 津山/津和野, lower-p60 岸和田/菊間, upper-p81 大溝/岡田) that had been flagged but not closed in the earlier 2026-05-19 audit. Direct crop inspection confirmed all three are clean in the current MASTER.

A separate impossible-state cell investigation caught four transcription errors in 森藩 that had been producing an impossible 0.99 persons/household ratio:

Field Was (wrong) Corrected to Error type
總戸數 12,645 2,645 Extra 一萬 prefix
士族戸數 218 128 Wrong leading digit (2→1)
卒族戸數 230 130 Wrong leading digit (2→1)
大豆 (koku) 205 1,205 Dropped 千 prefix

All four were verified by re-reading the high-resolution Hansei Ichiran vol 2 page (PDF p113-L). Post-correction the persons/household ratio resolves to 4.74 (normal).

HI-vs-primary discrepancy investigations of 4 cases where Hansei Ichiran differed from Meiji Shiyō by more than 2× confirmed all 4 are either primary-source-side errors (廣島’s 9,536 vs HI’s 97,536 — primary missing 萬 prefix) or unit-definition differences (泉’s 名 vs 人). HI is correct in all 4.

The audit also caught 3 build-script bugs that had been silently producing wrong values for 庄内 / 湯長谷 / 新庄 (the 人員 (status_class) prefix-match pollution of hi_jinkou), 鶴田 (military-row pollution of hi_shizoku_jinin), and 長州 / 郡山 (missing 内 X 人口 whitelist pattern). All fixed.

22.1.3 Layer 3 — Ravina replication audit

Mark Ravina’s Land and Lordship in Early Modern Japan (1999) Appendix Table A1 provides per-domain ratios (kusadaka / commoner, retainers per 100 commoners, gendaka (現高) / commoner, retainers per 1,000 koku, gendaka / kusadaka) for roughly 227 domains. The dataset computes the same ratios both from Hansei Ichiran directly and from primary sources (Ōkuma + Meiji Shiyō + Tōkei Shūshi), and audits the deltas against Ravina.

State after final pass (all 227 Ravina rows merged):

Match category Hansei Ichiran-derived Primary-derived
Clean match (< 20 percent Δ) 78 173
Close (< 50 percent Δ) 44 19
Divergent (≥ 50 percent Δ) 42 12
Incomputable 42 2
Not in Ravina A1 83 83

The primary-derived ratios match Ravina substantially better than the Hansei Ichiran-derived ratios (173 clean matches against 78). This reflects that Ravina himself worked from the same primary sources the dataset now cites — Meiji Shiyō and the Ōkuma papers — rather than from Hansei Ichiran directly. The 12 primary-derived divergent cases were individually diagnosed. The notable finding: Akita’s gendaka_per_commoner = 0.049 in Ravina is a confirmed decimal-place typo (should be 0.49 per the primary-derived value).

22.1.4 Layer 4 — The honest-medium tracker

The tracker records every value flagged at medium confidence — explicitly not claiming high-confidence accuracy, with a reason category and reason detail per row.

Starting at 141 medium-confidence rows, the tracker was reduced over a series of cleanup passes to 22 rows. The remaining 22 are structural source-level limits — no further crop re-reading or OCR work will resolve them. These cells affect tail-end fiscal precision and hyper-specific demographic sub-counts. None of them feed into the analytic dataset’s independent variable (kokudaka), dependent variable (kazoku rank), or any control variable.

22.1.4.2 The 22 remaining rows by category

Reason category Count Why it cannot be resolved further
Sub-koku precision 4 Sub-koku decimals (升 / 合 / 勺 / 才) below current crop resolution. The integer is correct; the tail digit is ambiguous between e.g. 二 and 三. Affects 4th decimal place only.
Unit ambiguity 4 Compound Edo-era monetary units (gold 兩 / 分 / 朱, silver 貫 / 匁, copper 文). OCR captures the digits but loses which unit each digit belongs to.
Dense-column ambiguity 6 Pages with 5–10 narrow columns of nearly-identical subtotals. Hard to tell which column owns a value at current resolution.
Needs higher resolution 3 Value is on the page; key characters can’t be discriminated at current resolution. Would resolve with higher-resolution re-photography at NDL.
Source illegible 5 Original ink in the 1870 print is too faded. No re-OCR can fix this; would require a different physical copy of Hansei Ichiran.

22.1.4.3 Concrete examples — one per category

  • Sub-koku precision: 七日市藩 内米 = 3,718.04245 koku — the integer 3,718 is confirmed; the .04245 vs .04235 fraction hinges on one ambiguous character. Doesn’t affect any analysis using koku-level precision.
  • Unit ambiguity: 佐賀藩 雜稅金 = 10 — could be 10 ryō (gold) or 10 koku-equivalent. The raw kanji are preserved but parseable only with manual interpretation.
  • Dense-column ambiguity: 山崎藩 雑税金 = 27.14086 — multiple gold sub-lines on the page; column attribution is not isolable.
  • Needs higher resolution: 厳原藩 寺院 = 199 — the page may actually read 287 (including 盲僧, blind monks); the scope-distinguishing annotation isn’t crisply readable.
  • Source illegible: 竜野藩 穢多 = 6 — the label is visible but the number is too faded to verify.

22.2 High-impact specific fixes

Beyond the audit-driven reductions, several specific fixes had material analytic impact and are worth flagging explicitly because they corrected values that would otherwise have entered analytic tables.

22.2.1 Sendai mislabel — “兵卒 18,913” was actually sotsu demographics

The single highest-impact fix. Sendai’s hi_total_troops_persons field had been populated with 18,913, but the underlying source row labelled “兵卒 (alt)” contained 5,171 women — an impossible mix for an active military unit. Re-examination showed the row was actually Sendai’s sotsu (sotsu-class) household population, not troops.

Corrected to:

  • hi_total_troops_persons = 14,262 (the correct active military personnel from the 兵士 員 row)
  • hi_sotsu_jinin = 18,913 (correctly bucketed as demographics)

A latent-mislabel audit was then extended across all military-bucketed rows; no other mislabels of similar magnitude were found.

22.2.2 Tosa 人口 = 896,808 — Satsuma’s page-spill (right-of-marker), re-attributed

Tosa’s Hansei Ichiran 人口 had been recorded as 896,808 — identical (within 9 persons) to Satsuma’s primary-source population. The 2026-05 right-of-marker sweep established this is not an OCR column-stack: the entire demographic block on upper_p089_R sits to the right of the ○高知藩 marker and is therefore Satsuma’s (Satsuma’s own page carries no demographics). Re-attributed to Satsuma; Tosa’s real Hansei Ichiran population — 495,936 (upper_p090_R) — now populates hi_jinkou, and Satsuma’s demographics (174,528 / 896,808 / 士族 43,119 / 102,949) were recovered. A physical page-spill, not a tabulation error.

22.2.3 Saga — absent from Hansei Ichiran’s military rosters

Saga (佐嘉藩) has a Volume 1 fiscal entry and a partial Volume 2 fiscal entry, but is completely absent from Volume 2’s military rosters — the standard tables of troop strength, officer rosters, and warships that every other major domain has. This is a source-level gap, not an extraction failure. The pipeline handles this via SOURCE_GAP marker rows; Saga’s military details come from a published-source supplement (藤野保 2010, Beasley 1972 — see the Saga walkthrough).

22.2.4 Stacked-page contamination — four confirmed cases

The four cross-page contamination fixes (Wakayama / Kanazawa, Tsuyama / Tsuwano, Kishiwada / Kikuma, Okada / Ōmizo) corrected 11 + 6 + 7 + 1 = 25 wrongly-attributed rows and added 12 + 6 = 18 newly-extracted verified rows. Cumulative impact on the analytic dataset: Wakayama’s kokudaka_iv went from a contaminated 1.35 million (Kanazawa’s value) to its true 539,469; Okada’s hi_jinkou went from 11,433 (Ōmizo’s value) to its true 16,231.

22.2.5 Akita Ravina A1 typo confirmed

Ravina A1’s value for Akita 久保田藩 gendaka_per_commoner is 0.049, which is implausibly low. Primary-derived computation gives 0.457. The dataset uses either 0.49 (the corrected Ravina value, treating the printed 0.049 as a decimal-place typo) or the primary-derived 0.457, and notes the discrepancy explicitly.

22.2.6 Four domain-category reclassifications

Surfaced during the re-sourcing pass that replaced the bakumatsu-website seed values with academic citations:

  • 仙台藩 (Date): originally coded shinpan (親藩) → corrected to tozama per Beasley p. xii and Kokushi daijiten
  • 伊予吉田藩 (Date cadet): shinpan → tozama
  • 対馬府中藩 (Sō): shinpan → tozama (kokushukaku (国主格) status but tozama (外様) by family origin)
  • 桑名藩 (Hisamatsu-Matsudaira): fudai (譜代) → shinpan (Hisamatsu-Matsudaira consistency with 伊予松山 and 今治)

Discussed in detail in the methodology note in chapter 03c.

22.2.7 Five Boshin alignment reclassifications

Also from the re-sourcing pass:

  • 守山藩, 館藩 (= 松前): originally coded pro-bakufu (幕府) → corrected to anti-bakufu (both are Northern Alliance defectors per 工藤威 2002)
  • 村上藩, 村松藩: originally coded anti-bakufu → corrected to pro-bakufu (both are Northern Alliance combatants per 工藤威 2002)
  • 淀藩: originally coded neutral → corrected to anti-bakufu (castle-gate refusal at Toba-Fushimi 1868-01-28)

22.3.1 2026-05-30 corrections (native-350-DPI re-reads)

A targeted re-read of three Hansei Ichiran entries at native resolution corrected three issues. The rebuilt dataset stays at 286 rows; a second rebuild produced zero further changes (the build is idempotent), and the four focal domains’ headline figures were unaffected.

  • 佐賀 (Saga, focal) — the “military gap” was wrong. Saga does have a Volume 2 Table-9 military entry (2,972 troops / 36 cannon, book pp. 429–430) — it had been missed because Table 9 prints the modern 佐賀 while earlier searches used the old-kanji 佐嘉. The SOURCE_GAP rows were removed and the military-omitted / warships-absent flags cleared. Hansei Ichiran is now a citable primary for Saga’s army (the navy still comes from a published source). See the Saga walkthrough.

  • 庄内 / 大泉 / 村岡 — a chimera + a duplicate. Hansei Ichiran records Shōnai under its 1869 name 大泉藩 (120,000 koku). A separate “庄内藩” row had been built from an entry mis-read as 鶴岡藩 — actually 邨岡藩 (村岡藩, Muraoka), an unrelated 11,000-koku tozama Yamana domain in Tajima whose HI numbers had been fused with Shōnai’s external identity. Result: Shōnai had been counted twice and Muraoka not at all. The fix de-duplicates Shōnai (single canonical 庄内藩, 譜代 / pro-bakufu / count) and restores 村岡藩 as its own row (外様 / pro-imperial / baron, 但馬/兵庫県).

  • 岡山 (Okayama) — cross-page troop mis-attribution. Okayama’s recorded troops of 141 were the 銃士121 + 砲士20 tail of the previous domain (沼田藩 / Numata) that prints just right of the ○岡山藩 marker; Okayama’s real army (十七大隊 / 7,862 men) sits on the next page. Corrected to 7,862; the 141 tail re-tagged to Numata. A reminder that the cross-page one-domain shift can hit even large, focal-adjacent domains.

Full audit trail: CORRECTIONS_LOG.md (2026-05-30 entry); apply script apply_saga_muraoka_okayama_2026_05_30.py; backups *.bak_pre_saga_muraoka_okayama_2026_05_30.

22.3.2 2026-05-30 (continued) — full right-of-marker sweep, PRESERVE-staleness reconciliation, and an OCR digit-inflation batch

Three further passes that session.

Right-of-marker sweep (all ~287 domains). Beyond the Tosa–Satsuma page-spill (above), one more cross-page troop mis-file: gosanke 和歌山 (Wakayama, 539k koku) had carried troops = 93, which is actually neighbouring 小見川 (Omigawa, ~10k koku)’s value, sitting just after the ○小見川藩 marker on the shared Table-8 page. Cleared — Wakayama’s own Table-8 block is officials-only; Omigawa keeps 93.

PRESERVE-staleness reconciliation. The build script overlays the previous analytic_wide.csv value back over freshly-computed values for ~14 Hansei Ichiran columns (to protect manual curation) — but that also silently re-imposed stale values after a later MASTER correction. Reconciling fresh-vs-preserved fixed 14 cells, including two focal: 佐賀 (Saga) population 358,458 → 354,458 (a user-approved OCR fix that had never propagated), and 薩摩 (Satsuma) troops 1,568 → blank (the 1,568 was Kanazawa’s orphaned value; Satsuma’s Table 8 is 書ナラス).

22.4 What the audit framework establishes

Three framings the dataset supports:

  1. Replication-audited primary-source coverage: every analytically relevant variable has either a citable primary source or a citable scholarly-synthesis source; the values were cross-validated against Ravina (1999) at the ratio level; cross-page contamination was systematically audited and the four surfaced cases were corrected.

  2. Transparent honest-medium tracking: the dataset maintains a per-cell tracker documenting every value that is not high-confidence, with the reason. Most empirical Meiji-history work either doesn’t disclose this or absorbs it silently; the present dataset surfaces it.

  3. Documented cleanup trajectory: the cumulative reduction from 141 medium-confidence rows to 22 is recorded with per-row audit trail; a reviewer can trace any specific value’s provenance back to the source crop and to the cleanup decision that affected it.

Audit infrastructure summary:

  • Layer 1 (within-domain ratio sanity) — a ratio-based outlier detector; current state in sanity_check_audit.csv released alongside the dataset.
  • Layer 2 (cross-page contamination) — exact-match audit (stacked_page_contamination_audit.md) plus fuzzy near-match audit; documented contamination cases listed above.
  • Layer 3 (Ravina cross-validation) — ravina_replication_audit.csv per-domain delta against Ravina A1, with diagnoses for the 12 divergent cases.
  • Layer 4 (medium-confidence tracker) — honest_medium_tracker.csv with reason categories per row; cumulative trajectory in honest_medium_tracker_summary.md.

A single per-column provenance file (data_provenance.md) consolidates citation guidance, coverage rates, and known caveats for every variable.
