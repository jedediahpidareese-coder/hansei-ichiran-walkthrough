22  Data quality and the audit framework

Any analytic result built on analytic_wide.csv is only as defensible as the values it ingests. This chapter walks the four-layer audit framework used to build confidence in the dataset, the cumulative reduction in known issues, the high-impact fixes that landed, and what remains uncertain — documented openly rather than hidden.

ImportantThe headline numbers
  • Master extraction record: 7,132 rows; 96 percent high-confidence (6,849 rows); 140 medium-confidence flagged; 0 low; 143 not applicable.
  • Honest-medium tracker: started at 141 flagged rows; reduced through successive cleanup passes to 22 rows, all of which are structural source-level limits that no further OCR work can resolve.
  • Within-domain sanity-check flags: started at 37; reduced to 26 after six confirmed corrections plus one alias-rule regression fix.
  • Cross-page contamination audits: 4 cases confirmed and fixed; a fuzzy near-match audit found 8 candidates, all benign.
  • Citation coverage on paper-critical variables: 100 percent on the three paper controls (kazoku (華族かぞく) rank, Boshin alignment, domain category); 99 percent on kazoku_rank_actual (the 3 missing values are documented exclusions, not data gaps).

The paper-critical variables are all paper-ready. The remaining medium-confidence rows affect tail-end fiscal precision and hyper-specific demographic sub-counts that aren’t used in any paper table or regression.

22.1 The four audit layers

22.1.1 Layer 1 — Within-domain ratio sanity

For each domain the audit computes ratios that ought to make demographic sense and flags any that don’t:

Ratio Plausible range Flag trigger
Average household size (人口じんこう / 戸數こすう) 3–7 persons Outside 3–7
shizoku (士族しぞく) persons per household 3–8 persons Outside 3–8
sotsu (卒族そつぞく) persons per household 3–8 persons Outside 3–8
Population per koku 0.5–5 persons / koku Outside 0.5–5
Status-class share of total population Each less than 50% Implausible class shares

When a flag fires, the audit identifies a specific root cause: OCR misread, mislabelling (sub-total mistaken for total), cross-page contamination, or genuine demographic anomaly.

Notable resolutions from this audit:

Domain Issue Resolution
一宮藩いちのみやはん 人口じんこう = 3,201 (impossibly far below the population-per-koku floor) OCR misread of 14,240; corrected via crop re-read
加納藩かのうはん 戸數こすう = 317 reported as the domain total Was actually the 城下町じょうかまち (castle-town) subset; relabelled
亀田藩かめだはん shizoku population = 23,258 Subtotal mislabelled; correct domain-wide total of 23,894 added
成羽藩なりわはん 人口じんこう = 494 Was 人員じんいん(寺領分じりょうぶん) — temple-land subset; relabelled
吉見よしみ藩はん 人口じんこう = 372 Was 人員じんいん 士卒族そつぞく — combined sub-class subset; relabelled
仙台藩せんだいはん A “兵卒へいそつ 18,913 persons” row that contained 5,171 women Was the sotsu (sotsu-class) household population, not active troops; relabelled. Active troops corrected to 14,262.
前橋藩まえばしはん shizoku household count equal to shizoku person count (4,656) Alias-rule regression — the bare “shizoku” prefix matched “shizoku (人口じんこう)”; alias rule corrected

The audit started at 37 flags and dropped to 26 after these fixes plus the contamination resolutions below. The remaining 26 flags are documented as definitional (for example 人吉藩ひとよしはん’s high sotsu ratio is real: Hitoyoshi-Sagara had a vast sotsu-class peasant-soldier population, confirmed against Meiji Shiyō).

22.1.2 Layer 2 — Cross-page contamination audits

Hansei Ichiran is a printed book; many of its pages contain two domains stacked vertically (upper and lower halves of a page). The extraction pipeline assigns rows to one canonical domain per page based on the visible 藩はん header. When a page has two domains, the assignment can drift — values from the lower domain accidentally tagged as the upper, or vice versa.

22.1.2.1 2a — Exact-match audit

The exact-match audit identifies pages containing two or more domains, then checks whether numeric values match exactly across the suspect pairs. 261 PDF pages have two or more domains. The audit found 22 value-collisions; 4 were confirmed contamination and fixed:

Page Pre-fix issue Resolution
upper-p87 和歌山わかやま / 金澤さわ 11 Wakayama rows duplicated Kanazawa data (Wakayama’s sōdaka (草高くさだか) wrongly showed 1,353,352 — Kanazawa’s value) Removed; Wakayama now correctly shows sōdaka = 539,469 (true Kii kokudaka (石高こくだか))
upper-p151 津山つやま / 津和野つわの 6 Tsuyama rows were a mix of Tsu (人口じんこう 247k) and Tsuwano (sōdaka 75k) All 6 removed; Tsuyama’s true sōdaka = 104,576 was already on p150 (unaffected)
lower-p60 岸和田きしわだ / 菊間きくま 7 Kishiwada rows duplicated Kikuma; Kishiwada’s actual data on p58 had never been extracted 7 wrong rows removed; 12 verified Kishiwada rows added from the correct crop
upper-p81 大溝おおみぞ / 岡田おかだ 岡田藩おかだはん’s 人口じんこう = 11,433 was actually Ōmizo’s value; Okada’s real population (16,231) had never been extracted 1 wrong row removed; 6 verified Okada rows added from the correct crop

22.1.2.2 2b — Fuzzy near-match audit

A complementary methodology with 0.5 percent relative tolerance — catches contamination where the values aren’t exactly equal but are very close (for example two adjacent 10,000-koku-tier domains reporting similar values). Found 8 candidates; all 8 verified as benign:

  • 5 cases: sequential book page numbers showing up as similar values across nearby pages — incidental, not data contamination.
  • 1 case: small-number coincidence (both values in the 100–200 range).
  • 2 cases: Tateyama vs Tawaramoto (both 10,000-koku domains) and Himeji vs Hikone (both very large sub-territory tallies) — confirmed as legitimate independent values that happen to be coincidentally close.

22.1.2.3 2c — The Tosa-Satsuma column-stack

The most-historically-instructive contamination wasn’t caught by either intra-Hansei Ichiran audit because it involves an external primary source. Tosa’s hi_jinkou value of 896,808 is essentially identical to Satsuma’s population_tokei_shushi value of 896,817 — a column-stack OCR error in the Tōkei Shūshi tabulation pipeline. The dataset substitutes Meiji Shiyō’s 516,867 for Tosa’s population.

A cross-source contamination audit (Hansei Ichiran versus primary-source) would catch this pattern systematically; the current dataset handles the Tosa case as a documented one-off correction.

22.1.3 Layer 3 — Ravina replication audit

Mark Ravina’s Land and Lordship in Early Modern Japan (1999) Appendix Table A1 provides per-domain ratios (kusadaka / commoner, retainers per 100 commoners, gendaka (現高げんだか) / commoner, retainers per 1,000 koku, gendaka / kusadaka) for roughly 227 domains. The dataset computes the same ratios both from Hansei Ichiran directly and from primary sources (Ōkuma + Meiji Shiyō + Tōkei Shūshi), and audits the deltas against Ravina.

State after final pass (all 227 Ravina rows merged):

Match category Hansei Ichiran-derived Primary-derived
Clean match (< 20 percent Δ) 78 173
Close (< 50 percent Δ) 44 19
Divergent (≥ 50 percent Δ) 42 12
Incomputable 42 2
Not in Ravina A1 83 83

The primary-derived ratios match Ravina substantially better than the Hansei Ichiran-derived ratios (173 clean matches against 78). This reflects that Ravina himself worked from the same primary sources the dataset now cites — Meiji Shiyō and the Ōkuma papers — rather than from Hansei Ichiran directly. The 12 primary-derived divergent cases were individually diagnosed. The notable finding: Akita’s gendaka_per_commoner = 0.049 in Ravina is a confirmed decimal-place typo (should be 0.49 per the primary-derived value).

22.1.4 Layer 4 — The honest-medium tracker

The tracker records every value flagged at medium confidence — explicitly not claiming high-confidence accuracy, with a reason category and reason detail per row.

Starting at 141 medium-confidence rows, the tracker was reduced over a series of cleanup passes to 22 rows. The remaining 22 are structural source-level limits — no further crop re-reading or OCR work will resolve them. These cells affect tail-end fiscal precision and hyper-specific demographic sub-counts. None of them feed into the analytic dataset’s independent variable (kokudaka), dependent variable (kazoku rank), or any control variable.

22.1.4.1 The cleanup trajectory in summary

Pass Cases resolved Result Description
Pre-cleanup baseline — 141 Initial flagged-row count
First pass 1 140 One mis-attribution corrected (郡上ぐじょう → 久留里くるり)
Second pass 12 128 Multi-domain batch — OCR, relabelling, copy-paste fixes
Third pass 73 55 Largest single pass — batch OCR review across the “Other” reason category
Fourth pass 20 35 Cross-source verification fixes; 館藩たてはん (Matsumae) demographic cluster
Fifth pass 3 32 Quick-win OCR fixes
Sixth pass 7 25 Careful crop re-reads
Final pass 3 22 Two further structural fixes

Cumulative: 141 → 22 rows, a 119-row reduction (84 percent). Each pass’s specific fixes are documented in git commit history and in a per-row tracker-history file released alongside the dataset.

22.1.4.2 The 22 remaining rows by category

Reason category Count Why it cannot be resolved further
Sub-koku precision 4 Sub-koku decimals (升 / 合 / 勺 / 才) below current crop resolution. The integer is correct; the tail digit is ambiguous between e.g. 二 and 三. Affects 4th decimal place only.
Unit ambiguity 4 Compound Edo-era monetary units (gold 兩りょう / 分 / 朱, silver 貫かん / 匁もんめ, copper 文). OCR captures the digits but loses which unit each digit belongs to.
Dense-column ambiguity 6 Pages with 5–10 narrow columns of nearly-identical subtotals. Hard to tell which column owns a value at current resolution.
Needs higher resolution 3 Value is on the page; key characters can’t be discriminated at current resolution. Would resolve with higher-resolution re-photography at NDL.
Source illegible 5 Original ink in the 1870 print is too faded. No re-OCR can fix this; would require a different physical copy of Hansei Ichiran.

22.1.4.3 Concrete examples — one per category

  • Sub-koku precision: 七日市藩なのかいちはん 内米うちまい = 3,718.04245 koku — the integer 3,718 is confirmed; the .04245 vs .04235 fraction hinges on one ambiguous character. Doesn’t affect any analysis using koku-level precision.
  • Unit ambiguity: 佐賀藩さがはん 雜稅金ざつぜいきん = 10 — could be 10 ryō (gold) or 10 koku-equivalent. The raw kanji are preserved but parseable only with manual interpretation.
  • Dense-column ambiguity: 山崎藩やまさきはん 雑税金ざつぜいきん = 27.14086 — multiple gold sub-lines on the page; column attribution is not isolable.
  • Needs higher resolution: 厳原藩いわはらはん 寺院じいん = 199 — the page may actually read 287 (including 盲僧もうそう, blind monks); the scope-distinguishing annotation isn’t crisply readable.
  • Source illegible: 竜野藩たつのはん 穢多えた = 6 — the label is visible but the number is too faded to verify.

22.2 High-impact specific fixes

Beyond the audit-driven reductions, several specific fixes had material analytic impact and are worth flagging explicitly because they corrected values that would otherwise have entered analytic tables.

22.2.1 Sendai mislabel — “兵卒へいそつ 18,913” was actually sotsu demographics

The single highest-impact fix. Sendai’s hi_total_troops_persons field had been populated with 18,913, but the underlying source row labelled “兵卒へいそつ (alt)” contained 5,171 women — an impossible mix for an active military unit. Re-examination showed the row was actually Sendai’s sotsu (sotsu-class) household population, not troops.

Corrected to:

  • hi_total_troops_persons = 14,262 (the correct active military personnel from the 兵士へいし 員 row)
  • hi_sotsu_jinin = 18,913 (correctly bucketed as demographics)

A latent-mislabel audit was then extended across all military-bucketed rows; no other mislabels of similar magnitude were found.

22.2.2 Tosa 人口じんこう = 896,808 — column-stack OCR error from Satsuma

Tosa’s Hansei Ichiran population value of 896,808 is an OCR column-stack error — it is identical (within 9 persons) to Satsuma’s primary-source population, indicating the tabulation pipeline mis-aligned a column. The dataset substitutes Meiji Shiyō’s 516,867 for Tosa’s population.

22.2.3 Saga — absent from Hansei Ichiran’s military rosters

Saga (佐嘉藩さがはん) has a Volume 1 fiscal entry and a partial Volume 2 fiscal entry, but is completely absent from Volume 2’s military rosters — the standard tables of troop strength, officer rosters, and warships that every other major domain has. This is a source-level gap, not an extraction failure. The pipeline handles this via SOURCE_GAP marker rows; Saga’s military details come from a published-source supplement (藤野保ふじのたもつ 2010, Beasley 1972 — see the Saga walkthrough).

22.2.4 Stacked-page contamination — four confirmed cases

The four cross-page contamination fixes (Wakayama / Kanazawa, Tsuyama / Tsuwano, Kishiwada / Kikuma, Okada / Ōmizo) corrected 11 + 6 + 7 + 1 = 25 wrongly-attributed rows and added 12 + 6 = 18 newly-extracted verified rows. Cumulative impact on the analytic dataset: Wakayama’s kokudaka_iv went from a contaminated 1.35 million (Kanazawa’s value) to its true 539,469; Okada’s hi_jinkou went from 11,433 (Ōmizo’s value) to its true 16,231.

22.2.5 Akita Ravina A1 typo confirmed

Ravina A1’s value for Akita 久保田藩くぼたはん gendaka_per_commoner is 0.049, which is implausibly low. Primary-derived computation gives 0.457. The dataset uses either 0.49 (the corrected Ravina value, treating the printed 0.049 as a decimal-place typo) or the primary-derived 0.457, and notes the discrepancy explicitly.

22.2.6 Four domain-category reclassifications

Surfaced during the re-sourcing pass that replaced the bakumatsu-website seed values with academic citations:

  • 仙台藩せんだいはん (Date): originally coded shinpan (親藩しんぱん) → corrected to tozama per Beasley p. xii and Kokushi daijiten
  • 伊予吉田藩いよよしだはん (Date cadet): shinpan → tozama
  • 対馬府中藩つしまふちゅうはん (Sō): shinpan → tozama (kokushukaku (国主格こくしゅかく) status but tozama (外様とざま) by family origin)
  • 桑名藩くわなはん (Hisamatsu-Matsudaira): fudai (譜代ふだい) → shinpan (Hisamatsu-Matsudaira consistency with 伊予松山いよまつやま and 今治いまばり)

Discussed in detail in the methodology note in chapter 03c.

22.2.7 Five Boshin alignment reclassifications

Also from the re-sourcing pass:

  • 守山藩もりやまはん, 館藩たてはん (= 松前まつまえ): originally coded pro-bakufu (幕府ばくふ) → corrected to anti-bakufu (both are Northern Alliance defectors per 工藤威くどうたけし 2002)
  • 村上藩むらかみはん, 村松藩むらまつはん: originally coded anti-bakufu → corrected to pro-bakufu (both are Northern Alliance combatants per 工藤威くどうたけし 2002)
  • 淀藩よどはん: originally coded neutral → corrected to anti-bakufu (castle-gate refusal at Toba-Fushimi 1868-01-28)

22.3 What the final dataset looks like

After the audit layers, the cleanup passes, and the targeted fixes, the dataset’s quality profile is:

Variable family Coverage Quality assessment
Independent variable: kokudaka_iv (composite) 96% Primary source per kokudaka_iv_source; high confidence
Dependent variable: kazoku_rank_actual 99% 3 missing values are documented exclusions (kaieki (改易かいえき), temple-land, etc.) — not data gaps
Control: domain_category 100% Cited (Beasley + 児玉こだま 1977); 4 mis-classifications corrected
Control: boshin_alignment_group 100% Cited (Asami, Esposito, Beasley, 工藤くどう); 5 mis-classifications corrected
Genmai (rank-threshold numerator) 89% Backed by Meiji Shiyō and Ōkuma papers
Population (primary) 90% Meiji Shiyō + Ōkuma + Tōkei Shūshi
Population (Hansei Ichiran direct) 77% Direct from Hansei Ichiran
Military details (Hansei Ichiran) 56% troops, 16% warships Sparse by Hansei Ichiran’s construction; the four focal domains have published-source supplements

The 96–100 percent coverage on every analytically critical column is the working summary of the data-quality work. The remaining gaps are documented and either structural (Hansei Ichiran’s military coverage was always sparse) or specific (the four missing kazoku rank rows are real historical exclusion events, not transcription failures).

22.4 What the audit framework establishes

Three framings the dataset supports:

  1. Replication-audited primary-source coverage: every analytically relevant variable has either a citable primary source or a citable scholarly-synthesis source; the values were cross-validated against Ravina (1999) at the ratio level; cross-page contamination was systematically audited and the four surfaced cases were corrected.

  2. Transparent honest-medium tracking: the dataset maintains a per-cell tracker documenting every value that is not high-confidence, with the reason. Most empirical Meiji-history work either doesn’t disclose this or absorbs it silently; the present dataset surfaces it.

  3. Documented cleanup trajectory: the cumulative reduction from 141 medium-confidence rows to 22 is recorded with per-row audit trail; a reviewer can trace any specific value’s provenance back to the source crop and to the cleanup decision that affected it.

Audit infrastructure summary:

  • Layer 1 (within-domain ratio sanity) — a ratio-based outlier detector; current state in sanity_check_audit.csv released alongside the dataset.
  • Layer 2 (cross-page contamination) — exact-match audit (stacked_page_contamination_audit.md) plus fuzzy near-match audit; documented contamination cases listed above.
  • Layer 3 (Ravina cross-validation) — ravina_replication_audit.csv per-domain delta against Ravina A1, with diagnoses for the 12 divergent cases.
  • Layer 4 (medium-confidence tracker) — honest_medium_tracker.csv with reason categories per row; cumulative trajectory in honest_medium_tracker_summary.md.

A single per-column provenance file (data_provenance.md) consolidates citation guidance, coverage rates, and known caveats for every variable.
