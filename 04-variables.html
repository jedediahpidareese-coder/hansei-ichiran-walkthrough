16  The variables

The dataset has 107 columns organized into nine logical families. This page is a reference; you don’t need to memorize it.

16.1 The variable families at a glance

16.2 The most important columns for the paper

16.2.1 Revenue base — 石高こくだか (column kokudaka_iv)

The composite 石高こくだか — the maximum of the available Hansei Ichiran fields (草高くさだか, 合高ごうだか, 表高おもてだか) plus the Ōkuma / Meiji-Shiyō 表高おもてだか fallback — per domain. This is the denominator in the paper’s headline retainers-per-1,000-koku fiscal-distress metric (see chapter 04b).

  • Coverage: 276/287
  • Range: ~2,000 to ~1,400,000 koku
  • Why composite: Hansei Ichiran reports different fields for different domains. Saga uses 貮口合ふたくちごう (= 合高ごうだか); Chōshū uses 草高くさだか; some small domains only have 表高おもてだか. The composite picks the most-comprehensive figure available.
  • Citation: a sibling source column records which field was actually used for each domain

16.2.2 1884 華族かぞく rank (column kazoku_rank_actual)

The peerage rank awarded in 1884, one of 5 tiers.

  • Coverage: 284/287 (3 documented exclusions)
  • Distribution: 3 prince, 15 marquess, 35 count, 222 viscount, 9 baron
  • Citation chain: 浅見雅男あさみまさお『華族誕生かぞくたんじょう』(1994) + 官報かんぽう 第307号 (1884) + 華族大鑑刊行会かぞくたいかんかんこうかい編 (1990) for princes
  • See the 華族かぞく Rank chapter for the full story.

16.2.3 Treatment — Boshin alignment (column boshin_alignment_group)

Each domain’s side in the 戊辰戦争ぼしんせんそう (Boshin sensō).

  • Coverage: 287/287
  • Values: anti-幕府ばくふ (bakufu) / pro-restoration, pro-幕府ばくふ, neutral
  • Distribution: 236 anti-幕府ばくふ, 41 pro-幕府ばくふ, 10 neutral
  • Citation chain: Esposito (2020) + 工藤威くどうたけし (2002) + Beasley (1972). See the Boshin Alignment chapter.

16.2.4 Paper-relevant — Genmai-rule deviations (column kazoku_rank_deviation_genmai)

The 19 大名だいみょう (daimyō) families whose actual 1884 rank diverged from the mechanical prediction.

  • Coverage: 19/287 (most domains land where the rule predicts)
  • Why this column exists: Asami (1994) argues the 1884 threshold operated on 現米げんまい (genmai) — post-Meiji-2 tax-reported rice flow — not 草高くさだか/表高おもてだか. Applying the 現米げんまい rule, 19 cases show real deviations. These are the paper’s core sample.
  • See The 19 Deviations chapter.

16.3 A note on military data sparsity

The military columns are interesting but sparse:

Gap state Count What it means
COMPLETE 8 Full troop/warship/artillery roster
PARTIAL 173 Some military data, some missing
HI_OMITTED 6 Source explicitly says 書ナラス (“not recorded”)
HI_OMITTED_PARTIAL 14 Mixed — some military fields recorded, some 書ナラス
NO_MILITARY_DATA 85 Volume 1-only domains — no Volume 2 military entry expected (small fiefs without standing forces)
SOURCE_GAP 1 Saga — absent from Volume 2’s military rosters

For the paper’s actual military analysis, the focal-4 supplements (Satsuma, Chōshū, Tosa, Saga) draw from published scholarly sources (Esposito 2020, Craig 1961, Roberts 1998, 藤野保ふじのたもつ 2010) rather than from Hansei Ichiran’s sparse military columns alone.

16.4 How to find anything

The single-source-of-truth is data_provenance.md — every column has its source, citation guidance, coverage, and known caveats. The next chapters tell the narrative; that file is the lookup, and it will be released alongside the dataset.

Reference: data_provenance.md is the per-column technical reference. Per-row citations are stored in adjacent *_source columns where applicable (e.g., kazoku_rank_source, boshin_alignment_source, domain_category_source).
